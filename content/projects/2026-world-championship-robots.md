## Overview

A highly complex competition robot with 10-ball capacity, rapid discrete scoring, capability to select how many balls are scored at once, automatic ball park using opponent's ball, deployable wing, 9" stroke puncher.

The major subsystems are:

- Chassis
- Lever (two-stage telescoping scoring arm)
- Intake
- Ball park
- Puncher
- Odometry
- Color sort

![Team smiling at Worlds](projects/2026-world-championship-robots/media/IMG_4242.jpg)

## My Contributions

As chief engineer, I owned the CAD for all of the robot. Some individuaul components were made by teammates, but for most components and at the subsystem or system level, I designed these robots.

The software subteam for this team was just me and one other teammate. I programmed the motion library that ran the chassis and built the integrated control software for the scoring arm, he focused on counting logic, sorting logic, driver controls, and autonomous routines. 

## Fabrication and Tools

- Diode laser
- CO2 laser
- FDM 3D printer (PLA, TPU, PAHT-CF)
## Results

- 11-2 at the World Championship
- Quarterfinalist
